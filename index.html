Features & Goals
Unique & Immersive Terrain
TerraForged is a complete overhaul of the Minecraft terrain generator. The focus is on creating worlds that look & feel expansive and interesting. Much of the terrain is based on real-life landforms & processes but the goal is not to make your Minecraft world more "realistic", rather "believable" or "immersive". The hope is that this encourages the explorers out there and inspires the builders!
Mod Compatibility
While TerraForged is a massive departure from Vanilla world gen, it is designed to adopt content provided by other mods (such as their biomes & blocks) automatically without configuration or additional compat mods. That said, deeper integrations are very much possible through TerraForged's API.

Erosion & Improved Rivers

A major component to the visual style of TF's terrain is in it's use of hydraulic erosion. This process traces the paths of thousands of water droplets down the slope of the terrain, simulating material erosion & deposition, and creates subtle lines and variations in the heightmap that look great! Terraforged also uses this erosion data to decorate terrain with certain blocks - such as placing gravel (scree) at the bottom of steep slopes and removing snow from locations where it wouldn't realistically settle.

Also in TerraForged, rivers go somewhere! Rivers always end at an ocean, gradually become narrower towards their start, and can stretch for thousands of blocks. Some even fork into two, sometimes multiple times, and some you may find great lakes at their source.

Custom Features & Decoration
TerraForged integrates a flexible system for loading custom features into the world, replacing/modifying existing ones, and controlling when/where they get placed. For example, TerraForged provides it's own set of tree assets to replace the Vanilla ones. It modifies snow layer placement to create spectacular, smooth, snow drifts.
Highly Configurable
Love sliders? TerraForged's got you covered! Control anything from how much ocean your world has to the heights of your river banks! All this within an interactive GUI with a handy preview-map showing just how your settings will affect the resulting world.
