Introduction

I've been work with Minecraft's terrain generation as a personal side-project for over 2 years, after stumbling down the rabbit hole that is procedural generation... In that time I've learnt many things & suffered many headaches!

My motivation has always been to generate Minecraft worlds I really felt like exploring - I'm a keen hiker IRL so combining that with my interests in programming & Minecraft has been a natural progression.

Happily, this all seems to have come together to a point where I think others might get some enjoyment out of it too, so I gave it name: TerraForged.

Author: dags

Features & Goals
Unique & Immersive Terrain
TerraForged's main goal is to create worlds that feel coherent, expansive and interesting. I don't particularly like the label "realistic"; preferring "believable" or "immersive". That said, some of the processes that go in to making the terrain are based (loosely) on real-life processes - partly because I liked the results, partly because they were fun to recreate/approximate.
Mod Compatibility

While TerraForged is a massive departure from Vanilla world gen, it has been designed largely to follow the same conventions that most biome & block providing mods adhere to and expect, so compatibility out of the box should be automatic with little to no configuration.

Deeper integrations will also be possible through TerraForged's API (coming soon).

Erosion & Improved Rivers

A major component to the visual style of TerraForged's terrain is in it's use of hydraulic erosion. This process traces the paths of thousands of water droplets down the slope of the terrain, simulating material erosion & deposition, and creates subtle lines and variations in the heightmap. Terraforged also uses this erosion data to decorate terrain with certain blocks such as placing gravel (scree) at the bottom of steep slopes and removing snow from locations where it wouldn't realistically settle.

Also, rivers go somewhere! Rivers always end at an ocean, gradually become narrower towards their start, and can stretch for thousands of blocks. Some even fork into two, sometimes multiple times, and some you may find lakes at their source.

Custom Features & Decoration
TerraForged integrates a flexible system for loading custom features into the world, replacing/modifying existing ones, and controlling when/where they get placed. For example, TerraForged provides it's own set of tree assets to replace the Vanilla ones. It modifies snow layer placement to create smooth snow drifts which look really cool.
Highly Configurable
Love sliders? TerraForged's got you covered! Control anything from how much ocean your world has to how common your volcanoes are! All this within an interactive GUI with a handy preview-map showing just how your settings will affect the resulting world.
